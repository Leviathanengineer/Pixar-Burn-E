Wiring Guide for the WALL-E Robot

### **Microcontroller**

* Arduino (Uno/Nano/ESP32/etc. depending on what you use)

---

### **OLED Display (SSD1306, I²C version)**

* **VCC → 3.3V or 5V** (check module, most support 5V input)
* **GND → GND**
* **SDA → Arduino SDA pin** (A4 on Uno, D21 on ESP32, etc.)
* **SCL → Arduino SCL pin** (A5 on Uno, D22 on ESP32, etc.)
* Address: `0x3C` (already set in code)

---

### **PCA9685 Servo Driver (16-channel PWM, I²C)**

* **VCC → 3.3V–5V logic power** (connect to Arduino VCC)
* **GND → Arduino GND**
* **SDA → Arduino SDA pin** (same as OLED)
* **SCL → Arduino SCL pin** (same as OLED)
* **V+ (servo power) → External 5–6V supply** (depends on your servos)
* **GND of external supply MUST connect to Arduino GND**

👉 Servos plug into PCA9685 outputs:

* Channel **0 → Left Arm Servo**
* Channel **1 → Right Arm Servo**
* Channel **2 → Head Servo**

---

### **Servos**

* Powered through PCA9685 (not directly from Arduino!)
* Each servo’s signal wire goes to its channel pin (0–2 above).

---

### **Buzzer**

* **Positive → Arduino D8**
* **Negative → Arduino GND**

---

### **Microphone Module (analog sound sensor)**

* **OUT (analog) → Arduino A0**
* **VCC → 5V**
* **GND → GND**

---

### **Button (voice activation trigger)**

* **One side → Arduino D2**
* **Other side → GND**
* Arduino has `INPUT_PULLUP` enabled, so no external resistor needed.

---

### ⚠️ Power Notes

* Servos draw too much current for the Arduino’s 5V pin. Use a **separate 5–6V power supply** for servo V+.
* Tie all grounds together (Arduino GND ↔ PCA9685 GND ↔ Servo power GND).
* If using more than 2–3 small servos, make sure your external supply can handle **2–3A** minimum.
